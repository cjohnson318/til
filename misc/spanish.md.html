# Spanish ## Verbs Work on learning present, preterite, and future first. Also learn the imperfect for ser and estar, these show up frequently in the news. The past participle The gerundio in Spanish, usually ending in -ando or -iendo, is used as a present participle in English, a verb ending in -ing. The participio in Spanish, is like the past participle in English. It is often used with haber as an auxillary verb. The preterite is a past tense that talks about somethign that happened one, at distinct time in the past. The imperfect is a past tense that talks about things that "used to" happen, for long periods of time, or with regularity. ### Short List of Irregular Verbs - Ser - to be (permanent) - Estar - to be (impermanent) - Haber - auxilliary to be, to have - Tener - to have - Ir - to go - Hacer - to make - Venir - to come - Salir - to leave - Dar - to give - Decir - to say - Querer - to want ### Longer List of Irregular Verbs - Pensar - to think - Creer - to believe - Saber - to know - Entender - to understand - Poder - to be able to - Ver - to see - Oír - to hear - Poner - to put - Llegar - to arrive - Traer - to bring - Encontrar - to find, to meet - Conocer - to get to know - Sentir - to feel, to feel sorry for - Volver - to return, to do again - Cerrar - to close - Empezar - to begin - Negar - to deny - Perder - to lose - Recomendar - to recommend - Costar - to cost - Recordar - to remember - Conseguir - to get, to obtain - Pedir - to ask for - Repetir - to repeat - Seguir - to follow - Servier - to serve ### Ser Used for permanent or essential attributes. #### Parts of Speech | PoS | Spanish | |-----------------|----------| | Past participle | sido | | Gerund | siendo | #### Examples of sido: He sido honesto con él.
